Dislike the appearance


There are multiple reasons you may be unsatisfied with your smile here are a few common ones:

Teeth look artificial - tooth selection is one of the most common reasons dentures look like dentures. Acrylic, plastic the teeth are made of, is different from your natural tooth, and there are multiple ways tooth manufacturers mimic the look of a natural tooth and therefore there are many different denture teeth you can select from when choosing your smile. The cheapest teeth that only have two layers and are made from a softer, lower grade acrylic usually have that artificial look. As the molds increase in quality and expense they have many more different layers to them and the exterior surface is much more contoured, textured, creating the look of a natural tooth. It is your decision as a patient how you wish your smile to look.

Things to look for when choosing teeth: 1. Opaque vs. translucent - cheap teeth have two layers only you can see color as uniform. Premium teeth have multiple layers so you can see a gradient of translucency when looking at the edges of the front teeth specifically - the more layers, the more natural is the look. 2. surface - cheap teeth will have very little texturing and appear flat. As the molds increase in quality more and more surface texture appears. If you run your finger on the surface of the teeth you can feel all the grooves, as well as the edge of the tooth is not flat, but is slightly rounded - how a natural tooth would be. 3. Hardness - Your dentures will last as long as your teeth will not wear out. Selecting a premium tooth mold often results in a longer lifespan of the denture. You still have to reline the denture regularly for the fit to be proper, but the longevity of the higher quality tooth denture will surpass that of a lower-quality one.

You are not showing your teeth when you smile or show too much - position of your teeth is very important to the final look of your smile. Within the limits of function this can be modified to suit you. When we make a new denture for you, make sure to specify what dissatisfied you in the previous smile and we can customize it further at the try-in appointment. However note that tooth repositioning outside the bounds of function can cause issues with your speech. However if the reduced smile is caused by wear - restoring the teeth to the original length will only improve the outcome.
