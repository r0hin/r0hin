Immediate Dentures


For cases with remaining teeth that have to partially or completely be extracted an immediate denture protocol can be planned. This allows for the patient to not go without teeth, it allows for a better healing and even though its a drastic process, it allows for the patient to start getting used to something new earlier.

It consists with tight collaboration with the dentist throughout the process

an evaluation is made by the dentist onto which teeth are going to be extracted
Denturist takes impressions, bite records etc. this may take one to two appointments. Teeth and color is selected, there even may be a partial try-in if there is sufficient area of already missing teeth.
Dentures are made before the day of extractions.
On the day of extraction the planned teeth are extracted, dentures are put in immediately to serve as a bandage.

There are particular instructions patient must follow right after the procedure:

The denture acts as a bandage, it limits the swelling and keeps the extraction sites away from food and forces in the mouth that may dislodge the newly forming clots.

do NOT take out the denture for the first 48 hours
after 48h mark take the dentures out gently, wash, brush with dish soap and water, rinse your mouth gently without spitting - let the water drain out of your mouth on its own and put the dentures back in
keep the dentures in at night until 3 day mark
rinse with salt water intermittently to help with the swelling
do NOT swish vigorously, spit, suck or smoke - any pressure may dislodge the denture and / or clots

At 3 day mark a post op appointment - see the denturist to see how the healing is doing and determine if any adjustment is necessary.

After this appointment take the denture out at night in a denture box filled with water or soaking solution.

At 2 week mark (or earlier if the dentures feel loose) Denturist will see you again for a tissue conditioner appointment - the bone resorption would have taken place and the space formed will be filled with a temporary soft liner to promote stability and healing.

As the process of healing commences more bone resorption will be taking place and repeat appointments to reapply tissue conditioner will be necessary.

After around 4-6 month, when the denturist determines the rate of resorption decreased a permanent reline is then undertaken. This completes the treatment.

Warning: because immediate dentures are designed based on estimated measurements and because it is hard to predict the extent of the bone resorption that will occur sometimes the changes that take place render the denture made sub-optimal. If it can be predicted, a temporary denture will be used in this process and then instead of a reline a new set will be made that will account for all the changes.
